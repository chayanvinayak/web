The Science and Spirituality of Meditation - Documentary

Filmed over a period of 5 years across 3 continents, this documentary provides a unique look at how meditation influences us physically, psychologically and spiritually - through a series of exclusive interviews featuring a spiritual guru who believes that meditation is the key to personal healing and transformation, scientific experts who share their insights based on their latest medical research and practitioners - some of whom are using meditation as means to well-being even in the face of terminal illness.

ABOUT THE DOCUMENTARY

This 30-minute documentary captures a journey of 5 years and 3 countries to look at a growing body of scientific research, spiritual teachings and personal stories to gain a deeper, holistic understanding of how meditation influences us. Its the first documentary of its kind to provides unprecedented visual explanations and exclusive interviews featuring:
 
 

Fahri Saatcioglu

Professor of Molecular and Cell Biology
at the University of Oslo, Norway

 
 
 
 

Shri Ram Lal ji Siyag

Yogic Guru, Founder, Patron of Adhyatma Vigyan Satsang Kendra in Jodhpur, India

 
 
 
 

Dr. Frederick Hecht

Professor and Research Director at the Osher Center for Integrative Medicine
at the University of California, San Francisco
 
 
 

Dr. Frederick Hecht
Professor and Research Director at the Osher Center for Integrative Medicine
at the University of California, San Francisco

A renowned professor and research director, who has built one of the largest and most productive cohort studies of early HIV infection in the world along with a research program that focuses on mind-body intervention (particularly yoga and meditation), shares what his team has observed in recent clinical trials that incorporate meditative and yogic practices.

Fahri Saatcioglu
Professor of Molecular and Cell Biology
at the University of Oslo, Norway

A molecular biologist who has spent over a decade researching with leading university around the world and published over 60 papers on the topic, provides insights on how meditation practices influence us biologically and physiologically.

Shri Ram Lal ji Siyag
Yogic Guru, Founder, Patron of Adhyatma Vigyan Satsang Kendra
in Jodhpur, India

Hailed as a contemporary saint and revered by thousands of disciples across India, Guru Siyag gave up his ordinary materialistic life in the mid-1980s to quietly spear-head a spiritual revolution. Today, even at the age of 89 years old, he continues to teach yogic and meditative practices to aid thousands of people at no charge.

About Producer

My name is Chayan Vinayak Goswami and I am an independent filmmaker and owner of G-Productions LLC based in Menlo Park, California.

I have funded this non-profit documentary myself and have no affiliations with any of the organizations mentioned in this documentary. For the past 5 years, I've worked with a small team of people to connect the dots behind the spirituality of meditation, which has been explained in ancient scriptures thousands of years ago, with modern day scientific research taking place around the world.
 
 

Why Meditation?

Meditation, a centuries-old practice that has been highly regarded for its ability to heal and transform both the body and mind, has seen a significant rise of people practicing meditation to maintain their wellbeing along with being increasingly accepted by the scientific community for the benefits it provides. However, have you ever wondered what takes place in our bodies and minds when we practice meditation and what do these transformations look like?

My documentary, the Science and Spirituality of Mediation, investigates the answer to these questions.
